# Review And Trust Strategy

## Product Principle

Reviews are the trust layer for LocalTrades. Public source verification proves a provider exists; reviews should prove that local residents had real experiences with that provider.

Do not copy Facebook comments or third-party review text into the site. Use Facebook and community groups only to invite people to submit original reviews directly.

## Review Model

Allow both review paths:

- Resident-first: a local resident can review any listed provider.
- Business-requested: a claimed business can invite past customers to leave a review.

To keep friction low, the review form should ask only for the essentials:

- First name
- Town/community
- Email address, kept private
- Business/provider reviewed
- Service used
- Star rating
- Short written review
- Approximate month/year of work

Optional later fields:

- Photo
- Invoice/job proof
- Phone number for follow-up

## Moderation Rules

All reviews should be manually approved before publishing, especially during launch.

Approve when:

- The review appears to come from a real local resident/customer.
- The review describes a plausible service experience.
- The tone is useful to future residents.

Hold or reject when:

- It looks like spam.
- It is a duplicate.
- It appears to be a competitor attack.
- It contains threats, harassment, or private information.
- It is too vague to be useful.
- It appears to be written by the business owner.

## Public Display Rules

Before a provider has approved reviews:

- Show `No local reviews yet`.
- Do not show fake star ratings.
- Do not show generated recommendation counts.

After reviews exist:

- Show `1 local review` or `2 local reviews`.
- Only show an average star rating after at least 3 approved reviews.
- Label stronger reviews as `Verified job` only when enough private detail/proof has been checked.

## One-Month Review Launch Plan

### Week 1: Prepare The System

- Replace all placeholder ratings with honest empty states.
- Create review submission flow.
- Create admin moderation queue.
- Define review approval/rejection checklist.
- Add `Be the first to review` prompts to profiles.

### Week 2: Seed First Reviews Manually

- Ask trusted local contacts to submit real reviews for providers they have used.
- Reach out to people who commonly recommend trades locally and invite them to submit directly.
- Start with 10-20 high-quality reviews across common categories.
- Keep all submissions pending until manually reviewed.

### Week 3: Business Claim And Invite Loop

- Invite selected providers to claim profiles.
- Give claimed providers a simple review invitation link.
- Ask each claimed provider to invite 3 past customers.
- Track which providers are actively participating.

### Week 4: Public Trust Push

- Post in local Facebook/community channels asking residents to help build a South Dundas local services review layer.
- Emphasize that reviews are original, moderated, and built for rural service-area discovery.
- Publish approved reviews gradually.
- Identify the categories where reviews are missing and target outreach there.

## Open Decisions

- Exact wording for review form intro.
- Whether email verification should require a confirmation click before moderation.
- Whether negative reviews require extra private detail before publishing.
- Whether businesses can publicly respond in the MVP or only after claim flow is live.
